## About

I develop the **Dynamic Quantum Markov Games (DQMG)** framework — a multi-layer architecture for adversarial multi-agent coordination with fluid team boundaries. The framework integrates Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman-Isaacs (HJBI) optimization, Markov game foundations, W-state entanglement, Belavkin quantum filtering, and entropy-triggered team reorganization governed by an order parameter κ. My dissertation applies DQMG to settings where agent coalitions must form, dissolve, and reform under adversarial pressure — bridging quantum information theory, dynamic game theory, and multi-agent reinforcement learning.

---

## Research Interests

- **Quantum Markov Games**: HJBI saddle-point optimization over quantum strategy spaces; W-state entanglement for shared coordination resources; Belavkin filtering for partially observable quantum environments.
- **Entropy-Triggered Team Reorganization**: Phase-transition-like coalition dynamics governed by an order parameter κ; nonstationarity modeling via two-state flip matrices (System III).
- **Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning**: Minimax Q-learning, policy regret minimization, no-regret learning in quantum games, graph quantum games (GQGs) and graph-local quantum coarse correlated equilibria (GL-QCCE).
- **Quantum Game Theory**: EWL/MW entanglement schemes, adversarial CHSH game formulations, quantum Nash and correlated equilibrium concepts.
